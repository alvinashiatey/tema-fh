T.E.M.A.

The prompt for the first year core class was centred around three homonyms–sight, site & cite–among which I chose to explore site. Initially situating myself as the site for inquiry, I later, through a number of self questioning turned my attention to Tema a place I call home. Tema is located 16 miles east of the capital city; Accra, in the region of Greater Accra, and is the capital of the Tema Metropolitan District. Constructed on the site of a former small fishing village, it was commissioned by Ghana’s first president, Kwame Nkrumah, and grew rapidly after the construction of a large harbour in 1961. The Greenwich Meridian Line (0°00’00.00”E/W) runs through the city. Dropping the pin to a more specific location within Tema, this project engages the Tema fishing harbour as a point of departure but also a point of return back home. A dialogue between land and sea, I juxtapose a 3d rendering of an ocean As a visual study this book borrows from the harbour photos of type on canoes put in dialogue with a 3d rendered ocean 3d rendering my own ocean. Making conversation between a 3d rendered ocean and the type. The dimensions of this book stems from that of a regular canoe from the dock.
